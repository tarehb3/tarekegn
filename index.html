MY photo Gallary

Memories of *Junior Elementary School Shambu* in the town of Shambu

Time Is Gold

A rural child waking up early in the morning, herding cattle, helping with household tasks, and, if there was more work, continuing until reaching school—this is nothing new. Often, even running quickly to school was part of their daily life. This is an experience many rural children have gone through. I am one of them. In 2009, this photo was taken for my Grade 8 ministry exam card. The picture holds a special story for me and brings back memories of *Junior Elementary School Shambu* in the town of Shambu. That is why I chose to share it. Generations come, stay for a while, and pass—but memories like these remain.

My Mom you are a light in the darkness

Mother, you are a light in the darkness. My own mother embodies the meaning of the word “mother.” Words alone cannot fully express her; her actions and love speak far more than I ever could. She gave me life and opened the world for me—this is no small wonder. She devoted herself to me; she placed her life in my hands. Her beauty, her time, her strength—she sacrificed it all for me. So often, she endured hardships for my sake, humbled herself, even setting aside the joys of her own childhood. Even my shortcomings bring her happiness. Today I am 30, but to her, I am still her five-year-old child. She believes in me; when she hears my voice on the phone, she feels peace and smiles—my siblings often tease me about that. Mother, you are everything to me. Please continue to be there, untouched by harm. I love you deeply. — Mother Taro 💛
